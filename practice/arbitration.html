Arbitration

General information on domestic arbitration, including clause review, invocation notices, appointment issues, statement of claim, defence, counterclaim, evidence, hearings, interim relief, award challenge, enforcement and settlement.

When This Matter Usually Arises

Arbitration issues usually arise when a contract contains an arbitration clause and a commercial, construction, supply, service, partnership, lease or business dispute has reached a stage where formal dispute resolution is being considered.

The first review should identify the arbitration clause, seat, venue, appointment procedure, limitation, notice requirement, transaction documents, payment trail, relief sought and whether urgent interim protection is needed.

Clause And Forum Review

Arbitration Clause

The clause should be checked for scope of disputes, number of arbitrators, appointment method, seat, venue, governing law, institution rules and pre-arbitration steps.

Invocation Notice

Before invoking arbitration, the demand, dispute description, clause reference, proposed appointment and supporting documents should be aligned with the agreement and facts.

Court Support

Depending on the stage, court support may relate to appointment, interim relief, evidence assistance, award challenge or enforcement.

Limitation And Date Sensitivity

Cause Of Action

Record the breach, demand, refusal, termination, invoice due date, final bill date, acknowledgement and any settlement communications. These dates may affect limitation.

Notice And Appointment

Invocation notice, reply, appointment communications, tribunal constitution and procedural orders should be preserved date-wise.

Award Stage

After an award, limitation for challenge, enforcement or execution should be reviewed promptly with the award date, receipt date and any correction or clarification applications.

Matter Flow

Invocation And Appointment

The arbitration clause, governing law, seat, venue, appointment procedure and notice requirements are reviewed before invocation or response.

Pleadings And Evidence

Claims, counterclaims, defence, documents, witness material, expert material if any, and correspondence are organized for the arbitral record.

Award And Post-Award

After an award, further steps may relate to compliance, settlement, Section 34 challenge, enforcement or execution depending on the outcome.

Documents To Keep Ready

Agreement

  • Signed contract and arbitration clause.
  • Amendments, addendums or extensions.
  • Jurisdiction, seat and venue details.
  • Pre-arbitration notice or negotiation clauses.

Claim Record

  • Invoices, account statement and claim calculation.
  • Performance, delivery or completion proof.
  • Correspondence, minutes and admissions.
  • Loss, damages or interest calculation.

Proceedings

  • Invocation notice and replies.
  • Appointment papers and tribunal orders.
  • Pleadings, evidence and hearing record.
  • Award, correction order or enforcement papers.
Interim Relief And Urgent Issues

Some arbitration-linked disputes require urgent review before or during the arbitral process. This may involve preservation of assets, goods, documents, project material, bank guarantees, security deposits, confidential information or the subject matter of the dispute.

Interim relief strategy depends on the arbitration clause, court jurisdiction, tribunal status, urgency, evidence and the nature of protection sought.

Common Mistakes

Ignoring The Seat

The seat of arbitration may affect court jurisdiction and post-award remedies. It should not be treated as a mere venue detail without review.

Weak Invocation Notice

A vague notice that does not clearly identify the dispute, clause, claim and appointment position can create avoidable procedural issues.

Unorganized Record

Arbitration is document-heavy. Claims, defences, evidence and calculations should be organized before pleadings are prepared.

FAQ

What should be checked before invoking arbitration?

The arbitration clause, governing law, seat, venue, appointment procedure, limitation, notice requirements, and available documents should be checked first.

What documents are useful in arbitration?

Contracts, arbitration clauses, purchase orders, invoices, account statements, delivery proof, correspondence, notices, minutes, and prior settlement records may be relevant.

What happens after an arbitral award?

After an award, further steps may involve compliance, settlement, challenge, enforcement, or execution depending on the award, limitation, and forum.
