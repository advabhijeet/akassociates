CHAMBERS OF AK - SEARCH VISIBILITY AND CLIENT ACQUISITION ROADMAP
Last updated: May 17, 2026

Goal:
Improve discoverability for high-intent legal searches while keeping the website compliant, useful, and conversion-focused. The site should help potential clients quickly understand practice focus, jurisdiction fit, required documents, and how to make a clean enquiry.

Current Positioning:
- Advocates & Legal Consultants.
- Homebase: Patna, Bihar.
- Primary practice signal: Patna High Court, District Courts and forums of Bihar.
- Secondary practice signal: selected Delhi courts/forums and Supreme Court matters.
- Limited UP/NCR signal: Noida, Ghaziabad and Allahabad High Court-linked matters only.
- Core practice pillars:
  1. MSME Recovery
  2. Arbitration
  3. Commercial / Consumer Recovery
  4. RERA / Property
  5. Cheque Bounce

Positioning Rule:
The website should not look like a broad all-practice law-firm directory. It should present Chambers of AK as a document-first legal chambers focused on MSME recovery, arbitration, commercial/consumer recovery, RERA/property and cheque bounce matters.

Location Rule:
Keep Patna High Court and Bihar courts/forums as the strongest public signal. Delhi should be included as a secondary search location. UP/NCR should be limited to Noida, Ghaziabad and Allahabad High Court. Avoid broad "UP and Delhi NCR" wording unless a page specifically explains that scope.

Phase 4 - Sitewide Internal Linking
Priority: High.
- Homepage should lead with MSME Recovery and Arbitration.
- Practice page should show only the five core pillars.
- Courts page should reinforce Patna High Court and Bihar courts/forums as primary.
- Case Enquiry should ask visitors to classify matters under the five core pillars.
- Legal insights should internally link back to relevant practice pages and case enquiry.
- Avoid prominent internal links to non-priority practices.

Phase 5 - Content Cluster For Legal Updates
Priority: High.
Recommended publishing priority:
1. MSME recovery and MSEFC practical guides.
2. Arbitration notice, clause, Section 34 and enforcement guides.
3. Commercial/consumer recovery guides.
4. RERA/property dispute guides.
5. Cheque bounce guides.

Each article should link back to:

- the case enquiry page;
- the document checklist page where relevant;
- one or two related articles.

Phase 6 - Conversion Improvements
Priority: High.
- Keep WhatsApp Enquiry and Email Chambers visible on the homepage.
- Keep Case Enquiry in the navbar.
- Add clear enquiry prompts on practice pages.
- Add short document checklists to reduce back-and-forth.
- Track clicks on WhatsApp, phone, email, case enquiry and contact links through GTM data layer events.
- Do not treat EmailJS direct form-send as a GA4 conversion unless a separate data-layer event is added and documented.

Phase 7 - Trust Signals
Priority: Medium.
- Keep About page professional and non-solicitation oriented.
- Keep disclaimer, privacy policy and terms linked in footer.
- Keep "informational only, not legal advice, no advocate-client relationship" language across legal updates and enquiry pages.
- Add structured data only where accurate.
- Avoid exaggerated claims such as best, top, guaranteed, specialist, expert in a way that creates compliance risk.

Phase 8 - Performance, Core Web Vitals And UX
Priority: High.
- Continue checking mobile views at 360px, 390px, 430px and desktop around 1366px.
- Maintain strong contrast: black and white theme with gold accents.
- Keep buttons readable on desktop and mobile.
- Keep JavaScript modular and avoid unnecessary page-wide logic.

Phase 9 - Measurement Rhythm
Priority: Ongoing.
- Weekly: check Search Console indexing status, sitemap status and page errors.
- Weekly: check Analytics traffic, device mix and top pages.
- Monthly: identify pages with impressions but low clicks and improve titles/descriptions.
- Monthly: add one or two useful legal updates.
- Quarterly: review practice-page rankings and expand content where search demand exists.

Important Note:
SEO takes time. The best route is not keyword stuffing. The site should answer real client questions clearly, load well on mobile, and keep each page focused on a specific legal problem, location or forum.
