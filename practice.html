Practice Readiness

Dates First

Most matters require a date chart before strategy can be assessed. Preserve notice dates, dishonour dates, invoice dates, possession timelines, limitation triggers, court dates and correspondence chronology.

Documents First

Complaints, replies, notices, agreements, ledgers, payment proofs, title papers, order sheets and official communications should be arranged in a clear indexed sequence before detailed review.

Forum First

The appropriate forum may depend on statute, contract, territorial facts, pecuniary value, project location, arbitration clause, court stage and the specific relief required.

Relief First

A matter should be assessed by the relief actually required: recovery, injunction, refund, possession, enforcement, defence, settlement, appeal, review of notices or document preparation.

Risk Review

Common risks include limitation, incomplete records, unclear liability, wrong forum, weak service proof, missing authorisation, unverified title papers and undocumented settlement terms.

Compliance Tone

The website content is informational. It does not advertise, solicit, promise outcomes or create an advocate-client relationship merely by browsing or sending an enquiry.

Location & Forum Priority

Primary Practice Focus

Patna High Court, District Courts of Bihar and Bihar-linked statutory forums remain the primary practice and website-positioning focus.

Bihar Forums

MSME Facilitation Council, Bihar RERA, commercial courts, consumer forums, district courts and execution forums may become relevant depending on documents, limitation and relief.

Delhi And Supreme Court

Selected matters may involve Delhi courts, Delhi forums or the Supreme Court of India depending on jurisdiction, procedural stage and professional feasibility.

Limited UP/NCR Scope

UP/NCR positioning is limited to Noida, Ghaziabad and Allahabad High Court-linked matters. Broad UP positioning is intentionally avoided.

Forum First

The correct forum depends on cause of action, contract, project location, statutory route, valuation, limitation and pending proceedings.

No Forum Guarantee

Forum selection or appearance cannot be promised from a short enquiry and requires formal review of facts and documents.
