MSME Delayed Payment Disputes

General information on payment recovery and defence in micro and small enterprise delayed payment disputes, including claim preparation, Facilitation Council proceedings, conciliation, arbitration, and enforcement issues.

Search Intent

MSME delayed payment matters usually begin with unpaid invoices, delayed buyer payments, ledger mismatch, supply disputes, or refusal to clear dues after goods or services are delivered.

AK Associates is based in Patna and assesses MSME payment recovery and response matters connected with Bihar, UP and Delhi NCR depending on parties, transaction records, council jurisdiction, and documents.

Legal Framework

Payment Timeline

The MSMED Act contains delayed payment provisions for micro and small enterprises. Where a written payment period exists, it cannot exceed forty-five days from acceptance or deemed acceptance.

Interest Basis

Where payment is delayed beyond the applicable date, the statutory framework refers to compound interest with monthly rests at three times the bank rate notified by the Reserve Bank.

MSEFC Route

Delayed payment disputes may be referred to the Micro and Small Enterprises Facilitation Council, where the process may involve conciliation and, if required, arbitration.

Matter Flow

Eligibility & Records

Udyam registration, enterprise status, purchase orders, invoices, delivery proof, ledger records, and communications are reviewed before any claim or response is prepared.

Claim Preparation

The claim should clearly identify the buyer, transaction, supply or service, invoice-wise amount, payment history, interest basis, and documents relied upon.

Council Proceedings

Proceedings may involve notice, response, conciliation and, where required, arbitration under the MSMED framework before the appropriate council.

Enforcement

Orders or awards may require further steps for challenge, execution, or recovery depending on the outcome, limitation, and forum.

Supplier And Buyer Sides

For Suppliers

The focus is on proving enterprise status, supply or service, invoice amount, acceptance, delivery, payment reminders, and the unpaid balance.

For Buyers

Response strategy may involve checking supply disputes, quality issues, invoice mismatch, payment already made, limitation, council jurisdiction, and contract records.

For Pending Matters

If a filing is already pending, the application, notice, reply, conciliation record, hearing status, award, challenge, or enforcement stage should be identified.

Common Documents

Useful documents may include Udyam registration details, invoices, purchase orders, work orders, delivery challans, completion records, email or WhatsApp communications, ledger statements, payment reminders, and acknowledgement of dues.

If you have already filed or received a council notice, keep the application, notice, reply, hearing records, settlement communications, order or award, and any execution or challenge papers arranged date-wise.

Checklist

MSME Records

  • Udyam registration certificate/details.
  • GST details, if relevant.
  • Business correspondence.
  • Claim amount summary.

Transaction Papers

  • Purchase orders or work orders.
  • Invoices and delivery challans.
  • Ledger statement.
  • Bank payment records.

Recovery Record

  • Payment reminders.
  • Email or WhatsApp acknowledgement.
  • Legal notice, if any.
  • Any council filing or order.
Common Mistakes

Unclear Claim Amount

Invoice totals, part payments, debit notes, GST, interest and ledger balance should be separated clearly before filing or replying.

Weak Delivery Proof

Claims become harder when supply or service completion is not supported by challans, emails, acceptance records, work completion proof or acknowledgements.

Ignoring Forum Stage

Conciliation, arbitration, award, challenge and enforcement are different stages. Strategy should match the current procedural stage.

FAQ

Can every MSME file before MSEFC?

The delayed payment route is generally connected with micro and small enterprises and valid registration records. Eligibility depends on documents and transaction facts.

Why is forty-five days important?

Under the MSMED framework, a written payment period cannot exceed forty-five days from acceptance or deemed acceptance. The actual date chart should still be checked carefully.

Can a buyer defend an MSME claim?

Yes. A buyer may raise legally available defences based on supply, quality, payment record, invoice mismatch, jurisdiction, limitation, or other transaction documents.
