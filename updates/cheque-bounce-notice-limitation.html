Cheque Bounce Notice Timeline

A practical information note on arranging dates, notice records, and documents before taking a step in a Section 138 cheque dishonour matter.

Informational Note

Why The Date Chart Comes First

In a cheque dishonour matter, the first useful exercise is not drafting. It is preparing a clear date chart. Note the cheque date, presentation date, bank return memo date, date of information from the bank, notice dispatch date, delivery status, reply date if any, and any payment made later.

What The Demand Notice Record Should Show

The notice file should usually contain the notice copy, address used, postal or courier receipt, tracking report, delivery record, returned envelope if any, reply received, and proof that the cheque and liability documents were preserved.

For The Drawer Or Accused Side

If you receive a demand notice, preserve the envelope, notice copy, date of receipt, reply, payment proof, account record, and the transaction background. Defence assessment may depend on liability, cheque issuance, payment, limitation, service, and settlement communications.
