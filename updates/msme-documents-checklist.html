MSME Delayed Payment Documents Checklist

Before sending an MSME delayed-payment enquiry or preparing for the Facilitation Council route, the record should be organized invoice-wise and date-wise. This page focuses on the documents that usually matter first.

Why The Checklist Matters

MSME delayed-payment disputes often become harder because invoices, delivery proof, Udyam records and buyer objections are scattered across email threads, WhatsApp messages, ledger extracts and GST files. A clean document file makes the first assessment much more reliable.

1. Confirm Supplier Status First

Start with the supplier-side identity record. Keep the Udyam registration, business constitution documents, GST details if relevant, address record and proof showing that the enterprise status existed during the transaction period. If the business name changed, preserve the continuity documents.

2. Prepare An Invoice-Wise Recovery Sheet

A useful working sheet usually includes invoice number, invoice date, amount, due date, purchase order or work order reference, delivery proof, part-payment, unpaid balance and the latest buyer communication. If services were supplied instead of goods, identify the milestone or completion basis for each invoice.

Where there are multiple invoices, one mixed total figure is usually not enough. Keep the record invoice-wise so that supply, acceptance, payment and objection can be checked line by line before choosing a notice, settlement or MSEFC route.

3. Preserve Delivery And Acceptance Proof

Keep purchase orders, work orders, invoices, delivery challans, e-way bills where relevant, completion certificates, email acceptances and site records in one place. Weak delivery proof is a common reason why supplier-side claims become harder to present clearly.

4. Keep Buyer Objections In Sequence

Do not keep only reminders. Also preserve debit notes, quality objections, quantity disputes, reconciliation emails, ledger mismatch allegations, return or rejection records, and any admission or part-payment. These communications help assess whether the objection is contemporaneous, partial, afterthought, or tied to a specific invoice.

5. MSEFC Readiness Checklist

Before preparing for the Facilitation Council route, keep a short chronology with order date, supply date, invoice date, due date, first reminder, buyer objection, part-payment and the present outstanding amount. Also preserve the interest basis and any earlier legal notice or settlement communication.

6. First Enquiry Format

A useful first enquiry may include business name, Udyam status, buyer name and location, invoice-wise outstanding amount, delivery proof available, nature of buyer objection, part-payments received and whether any MSEFC, arbitration or court proceeding is already pending.
