Documents To Keep Ready

Title And Transfer Papers

  • Sale deed, gift deed, partition deed or agreement to sell.
  • Previous chain documents and encumbrance-related papers, if available.
  • Mutation, jamabandi, khata, khesra or revenue records.
  • Municipal holding, tax, map or building papers.

Possession And Site Records

  • Electricity, water, tax, rent or occupation records.
  • Photographs or videos showing possession, boundary or obstruction.
  • Site map, survey papers or measurement records.
  • Police complaint, local authority record or panchayat material, if any.

Dispute And Case Papers

  • Legal notices and replies.
  • Existing plaint, written statement, orders, appeal or execution papers.
  • Family settlement, correspondence or partition communications.
  • Timeline of events and current court stage.
Reliefs Commonly Assessed

Depending on the facts and documents, a property dispute may involve injunction, declaration, partition, specific performance, possession, cancellation of documents, damages, execution, appeal or settlement-oriented steps.

The procedural route depends on property location, cause of action, limitation, parties, valuation, possession status, title record and forum jurisdiction. This page is informational and does not promise any outcome or immediate relief.

First Enquiry Format

Send a short note with property district and village/city, nature of dispute, current possession status, title documents available, important dates, opposite party details, whether any notice or case is already pending, and whether urgent interim relief is required.
