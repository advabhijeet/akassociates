RERA Delayed Possession: Refund, Interest or Possession?

When possession is delayed, the first question is not only what relief is available. The practical question is what the buyer wants now, what the agreement records show, and whether the project timeline supports refund, interest, possession-oriented relief or settlement discussions.

Relief Choice

The refund-versus-interest decision should be document-led. It usually turns on project status, promised possession date, revised timelines, payment compliance, builder communications, registration details and the buyer's present objective.

Start With The Project Timeline

Prepare a date-wise chronology from booking to the latest builder communication. Include booking date, allotment date, agreement date, promised possession date, revised possession dates, demand letters, payments, construction updates, offer of possession if any and cancellation or settlement discussions.

If the project has multiple revised dates, separate the original contractual date from later emails, circulars, advertisements, portal updates or informal assurances. A clean chronology helps assess delay, builder conduct and the relief being considered.

Refund, Interest Or Possession

Some buyers want to exit the project and consider refund-oriented relief. Others still want the unit and focus on possession with delay interest, compensation or completion-related directions. The practical choice may depend on project stage, delay length, buyer finances, builder conduct, market conditions, loan status and whether the buyer still wants the specific unit.

Do not decide the relief only from frustration or pressure. A refund route, possession route or settlement route should be considered after reviewing payment compliance, possession offer, completion status, cancellation clauses and previous communications.

Documents To Keep Ready

  • Booking form, allotment letter and builder-buyer agreement.
  • Payment plan, receipts, bank statements and loan disbursement records.
  • Demand letters, reminders, construction updates and possession letters.
  • Project registration details and public project information, if available.
  • Emails, WhatsApp messages, notices and builder replies.
  • Cancellation, refund, alternate unit or settlement communications.
  • Photographs, site-visit notes or association communications, where relevant.

Payment And Possession Checklist

A practical file should show total sale consideration, amount paid, amount demanded, instalment schedule, due dates, loan status, tax/charge demands, promised possession date, offer of possession if any, current construction status and delay period.

If additional charges were demanded, identify whether they relate to maintenance, parking, club, GST, interest, escalation or possession-stage dues. These details can affect the enquiry assessment.

Builder Communications

Preserve the full communication trail, not just the most recent message. Earlier assurances, revised handover dates, acknowledgement of delay, settlement offers, refund promises and possession-related emails may all matter. Keep screenshots with dates and, where possible, export email threads as PDFs.

Forum And Project Location

RERA-related routes are commonly project-location based. Bihar is the chambers' home base, while builder-dispute matters may involve UP and Delhi NCR depending on the project and documents. Keep the exact project location, registration details, promoter name and authority-linked information ready before assuming the correct forum.

First Enquiry Format

A useful first enquiry may include builder name, project name and location, unit details, booking date, agreement date, promised possession date, total amount paid, current project status, possession offer status, loan status and whether the present preference is refund, interest, possession or settlement.
