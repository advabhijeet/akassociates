Arbitration Clause Checklist Before A Dispute

A general note on reading arbitration clauses before sending notices, filing claims or responding to contract disputes.

Informational Note

Read The Clause Before The Notice

Before invoking arbitration or replying to a notice, review the contract clause carefully. Important words may relate to seat, venue, governing law, appointment mechanism, number of arbitrators, language and institutional rules.

Check Court Support And Relief

Some arbitration-linked disputes may still require court support for appointment, interim relief, challenge or enforcement. The correct route depends on the clause, seat, relief and stage of the dispute.

Preserve The Dispute Record

Keep the contract, amendments, invoices, correspondence, breach notices, payment records, meeting minutes, termination letters and all prior settlement communications in order.
