Cheque Bounce Notice Timeline: Dates To Preserve

A cheque bounce notice enquiry should begin with the dates, bank record, notice proof and transaction papers. This guide explains how to prepare the timeline before taking any procedural step.

Why The Date Chart Comes First

In a cheque dishonour matter, the first useful exercise is preparing a clear date chart. Note the cheque date, presentation date, bank return memo date, the date when dishonour information was received, proposed notice dispatch date, delivery status, reply date if any, and any later payment or settlement communication.

If there are multiple cheques, prepare a cheque-wise table. Each cheque may have a separate date, amount, return memo, notice reference and settlement status. A single mixed summary can create confusion at the complaint or defence stage.

Demand Notice Record

The notice file should usually contain the demand notice copy, address used, postal or courier receipt, tracking report, delivery proof, returned envelope if any, reply received, and the papers showing the underlying transaction or liability.

Address verification is important. Preserve material showing the drawer's residential, business or registered address, such as agreement details, invoice records, KYC documents, email signatures, earlier notices or company records.

For The Drawer Or Accused Side

If you receive a demand notice, preserve the envelope, notice copy, date of receipt, reply, payment proof, bank account record and the transaction background. Defence assessment may depend on liability, cheque issuance, payment, limitation, service of notice and settlement communications.

Documents To Keep Ready

  • Original cheque or clear copy.
  • Bank return memo and account statement.
  • Demand notice, postal/courier receipt, tracking proof and returned envelope if any.
  • Invoice, loan paper, agreement, ledger, settlement note or liability document.
  • Messages, emails, acknowledgements and payment reminders.
  • Reply notice, settlement communications and part-payment proof.

Complaint-Readiness Questions

Before filing or responding, check whether the cheque was issued for a legally enforceable liability, whether the bank memo is available, whether the notice was dispatched to the correct address, whether delivery or deemed service can be shown, and whether the filing timeline has been preserved.

For company cheques, also keep the company details, signatory details, board or authorisation papers if relevant, and documents showing the role of the persons involved.
