Delayed Possession: Refund Or Interest?

When possession is delayed, buyers often ask whether the better first route is refund, interest, possession-oriented relief, or simply better document preparation. The answer usually depends on the agreement, timeline and project record.

How To Think About Relief

The refund-versus-interest question should not be answered only by preference. It usually turns on project status, promised possession date, revised timelines, payment compliance, builder communications and what the allottee practically wants at this stage.

1. Start With The Project Timeline

Prepare a date-wise chronology from booking to the latest builder communication. Include booking date, allotment date, agreement date, promised possession date, revised possession dates, demand letters, major payments, and site or project updates. This chronology usually helps frame both relief choice and forum discussions.

2. Refund, Interest Or Possession

Some buyers prefer to exit the project and consider refund-oriented relief. Others still want the unit and focus on possession with delay interest or compensation. The practical choice may depend on the project stage, delay length, buyer finances, builder conduct, market conditions and whether the buyer still wants that specific unit.

3. Documents To Keep Ready

Keep the allotment letter, builder-buyer agreement, payment plan, receipts, bank statements, demand letters, promised possession terms, project registration details and all emails, WhatsApp messages or notices exchanged with the builder.

If the builder offered a revised timeline, alternate unit, refund plan, cancellation route or settlement, preserve those communications in exact date order. They often matter more than generalized grievance statements.

4. Payment And Possession Checklist

A practical file should show amount paid, amount demanded, instalment schedule, possession promise, current construction status, offer of possession if any, delay period, cancellation discussions and whether any maintenance or additional charge was raised despite incomplete possession.

5. Forum And Location

RERA-related routes are commonly project-location based. Bihar is the chambers' home base, while builder-dispute matters may also involve UP and Delhi NCR depending on the project and documents. Keep the exact project location and registration details ready before making assumptions about the authority or next step.

6. First Enquiry Format

A useful first enquiry may include builder name, project name and location, unit details, booking date, agreement date, promised possession date, total amount paid, current project status and whether the present preference is refund, interest, possession or settlement discussion.
