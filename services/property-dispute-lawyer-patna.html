Property Dispute Lawyer in Patna

For property disputes, possession issues, injunction suits, partition, declaration, specific performance, and civil litigation connected with Patna or Bihar, this page explains the documents and first enquiry details that usually matter.

When This Page Helps

Title Or Ownership Dispute

You need to organize sale deeds, gift deeds, partition papers, mutation records, revenue papers, family arrangements, or other title documents before assessing civil remedies.

Possession Or Injunction Issue

You are facing interference, obstruction, dispossession threat, construction dispute, boundary issue, or urgent need to preserve possession or status quo.

Partition Or Specific Performance

The matter relates to family property, co-sharer rights, agreement to sell, delayed registration, refusal to execute documents, or enforcement of contractual property obligations.

Documents To Keep Ready

Title And Transfer Papers

  • Sale deed, gift deed, partition deed or agreement to sell.
  • Previous chain documents, if available.
  • Mutation, jamabandi, khata, khesra or revenue papers.
  • Municipal or tax records.

Possession And Use Records

  • Electricity, water, tax or rent records.
  • Photographs or videos showing possession or obstruction.
  • Boundary, map, survey or site documents.
  • Police complaint or local authority record, if any.

Dispute And Case Papers

  • Legal notices and replies.
  • Existing plaint, written statement, orders or appeal papers.
  • Family settlement or correspondence.
  • Timeline of events and current court stage.
Common Reliefs To Assess

Depending on the facts and documents, property-related disputes may involve injunction, declaration, partition, specific performance, possession, cancellation of documents, damages, execution, appeal or settlement-oriented steps.

The appropriate route depends on the property location, cause of action, limitation, parties, valuation, possession status, and forum jurisdiction. This page is informational and does not promise any outcome or immediate relief.

Route Review Before Filing

Before selecting a property dispute route, prepare a short issue note separating title, possession, mutation, boundary, family arrangement, agreement to sell, injunction and pending-case questions. This helps identify whether the immediate concern is preservation of possession, declaration of title, partition, specific performance, cancellation, execution or appeal.

Mutation and revenue records may be useful for possession and administrative history, but they should not be treated as a substitute for title-chain review. The enquiry should identify the property ward, village, khata, khesra, plot, municipal holding or survey details wherever available.

First Enquiry Format

Send a short note with property location, nature of dispute, current possession status, title documents available, important dates, opposite party details, whether any notice or case is already pending, and whether urgent interim relief is required.

FAQ

Do title papers matter?

Yes. Title, possession, mutation, revenue and municipal records usually form the starting point for assessing a property dispute.

Can urgent relief be considered?

Urgent relief depends on facts, possession, threat, documents, limitation and forum. A short timeline and documents help in assessing the next step.

Is this legal advice?

No. This page is general information. Advice depends on facts, documents, limitation, jurisdiction and formal consultation.
