Client Guide

Frequently Asked Questions

These general answers help visitors understand how to prepare an initial enquiry. Specific advice depends on the facts, documents, limitation period and applicable forum.

Before Contacting

What should I share in my first message?

Share the matter type, key dates, location, current stage, whether any notice or order has been received, and the documents currently available. Avoid sending originals at the first stage.

Can I send documents on WhatsApp?

For an initial discussion, scanned copies or clear photographs may be useful. Confidential or original papers should be shared only after the scope of consultation or engagement is understood.

Is a phone call enough to start a case?

A call can help understand the broad issue, but legal steps usually require document review, limitation checks, forum assessment and proper instructions.

Process

How are timelines assessed?

Timelines depend on the nature of the matter, statutory limitation, court or forum schedule, service of notice, documents, pleadings and conduct of the opposite side.

Can outcomes be guaranteed?

No. Legal outcomes depend on facts, evidence, law, forum procedure and judicial or authority decisions. The chambers can explain available legal options after reviewing the matter.

Which languages are supported?

Client communication can be handled in Hindi and English. Formal pleadings and filings are prepared according to the requirements of the relevant court or forum.

Trust, Engagement And Confidentiality

Does sending an enquiry create an advocate-client relationship?

No. An initial message, phone call, email or WhatsApp enquiry does not by itself create an advocate-client relationship. Formal engagement depends on conflict checks, scope, documents and professional acceptance.

Should I send confidential papers immediately?

No. At the first stage, share a concise summary, key dates and document names. Sensitive, privileged or voluminous records should be shared only after consultation or engagement is confirmed.

How are fees or scope discussed?

Fee, scope and next steps depend on the matter type, urgency, documents, forum, drafting requirement, appearance requirement and stage. These are discussed after understanding the basic facts.

Forums, Regions And Urgency

Which courts or forums are relevant?

The relevant forum may depend on the property/project location, contract clause, cause of action, valuation, statutory route, limitation, parties and whether a proceeding is already pending.

What should I mention for urgent matters?

Mention the next hearing date, deadline, limitation risk, notice reply date, dispossession risk, demolition/construction activity, warrant/summons issue, award challenge date or enforcement step.

Can the chamber handle all locations?

Every location or forum is assessed separately. Patna and Bihar-linked matters are central, while Delhi NCR, UP, Gurugram or other forum-linked enquiries depend on documents, procedural stage and feasibility.
